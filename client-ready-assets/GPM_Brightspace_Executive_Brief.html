Section 01

The problem is not tool cost.
It is operating drag.

GPM is not deciding between "cheaper tools" and "a more expensive LMS." The decision is whether learning can become a repeatable operating system — or remain a collection of manual workarounds that slow every team down.

Daily Headaches

Context switching at every step

Daily work depends on switching tools, moving data manually, and reconciling what should be automated. Every workflow touches multiple systems — and that friction compounds as volume grows.

Lost Revenue

Commerce and records are split

Selling, enrolling, tracking, and credentialing are not cleanly tied together. That creates friction at the exact moment a prospect becomes a buyer — and a buyer becomes a compliant, badged learner.

Scale Ceiling

Growth creates more coordination work

Every new course, partner, cohort, and reporting request adds more coordination overhead instead of more operating leverage. The current model penalizes growth rather than enabling it.

Leadership
Question
Can Brightspace remove enough operational drag to make learning revenue easier to grow, govern, and defend?

Section 08

Recommended executive demo flow

Five moves. Each one answers the same question: what manual work goes away, what revenue friction disappears, and what can leadership see sooner?

1

Start with the operating pain

Recap the current reality — five tools, manual handoffs, reporting debt, and revenue friction at every step. Make the pain visible before showing the solution. Leadership needs to feel the weight of the status quo before they'll value what replaces it.

2

Show workflow compression

Walk from course creation to sale to enrollment to completion to reporting — without leaving the operating model. Every step in one system is a handoff that disappears. Let that sink in.

3

Prove revenue and scale

Show Course Merchant catalog-to-cash, multi-seat or partner distribution scenarios, and where manual invoicing friction comes out. Quantify what that friction has been costing — in time and in dropped revenue.

4

Prove reporting confidence

Show the Performance+ executive dashboard: completion rates, engagement, revenue and program signals, and audit-ready records. The message: leadership gets answers in seconds, not after a Sheets rebuild.

5

Close with decision criteria

Align on what must be true for GPM to move from patchwork to platform. Name the criteria. Make the decision framework explicit. This sets the next meeting's agenda before you leave the room.

Avoid a feature tour. Make every click answer: what manual work goes away, what revenue friction disappears, and what can leadership see sooner? Features are props — operating outcomes are the argument.
